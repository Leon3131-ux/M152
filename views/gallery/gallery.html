Image Formats

JPG
  • JPG stands for “Joint Photographic Experts Group”, jpg is mostly used for product photos, human portraits, and other images where the color variances are important. JPG uses a compression method that removes colors that are visible with the human eye and through that decreases the file size.

  • Size: 16.7MB
PNG
  • PNG or “Portable Network Graphics” is one of the most common image formats if not the most common. It can display a wider range of colors. Due to the nature of this format, transparency is supported, and user can choose the degree. This means, that PNG is used when people are handling images that have transparency or if transparency is to be added.
  • Size: 37.2MB
GIF
  • At least we are to the GIF or also known as “Graphics Interchange Format”. GIF can display a sequence of images, that allows it to look like a short video. A downside to GIF is that the color is reduced to a range of 256, so small differences of the original pictures cannot be distinguished.

  • Size: 15.2MB

Video Formats

MOV
  • “QuickTime Movie” is almost the opposite of MP4 regarding quality. It stores higher quality video, audio and effects. The downside is that the file tends to be quite large compared to other ones.


  • Advantages
    • Higher Quality off audio and video
    Disadvantages
    • File is larger than other ones
  • Size: 28.1MB
MP4
  • MPEG-4 is the most common used video format. The name arrives from the algorithm it uses called MPEG-4, this algorithm stores video and audio files and offers a lower definition. MP4 is used to make short videos that don’t need a high grade of quality, perfect for YouTube, Twitter, Instagram and other applications like that.
  • Advantages
    • Relatively small file size compared to most formats
    • Easy use to make short videos
    Disadvantages
    • Low quality
  • Size: 18.9MB
WebM
  • Webm is an all-around good video file format, it has great quality of sounds and videos without compromising performance. Webm is specifically design for the internet, due to that it has a higher compression ratio to other video formats, such as MP4, that also allows it, to be smaller than a MP4 video.
  • Advantages
    • Good video and audio quality
    • Small file sizes
    • Good performance for even low-end machines
    Disadvantages
    • Not supported in Safari and Internet Explorer
  • Size: 17.3MB

Audio Formats

WAV
  • Waveform audio files (also called WAV files) are one of the more popular digital audio formats and a gold standard in studio recording. WAV was one of the first digital audio formats, and quickly became a staple across all platforms. Despite decades of progress, it still maintains its position as one of the world’s leading pro audio formats. (07.01.2022, https://www.izotope.com/en/learn/whats-the-difference-between-file-formats.html)
  • Size: 412KB
MP3
  • Uncompressed audio formats like WAV and AIFF provide gorgeous sound quality, but at the cost of high file size. With the boom of internet file-sharing in the mid-90s, people quickly realized sending uncompressed files over dial-up connections was impractical—and oftentimes impossible. Which is why MP3s (MPEG-2 Audio Layer III) were born. (07.01.2022, https://www.izotope.com/en/learn/whats-the-difference-between-file-formats.html)
  • Size: 72KB
OGG
  • OGG is a rarely used audio format and is described as “a multimedia container format”. Ogg is a container file format for multimedia files, so it can contain audio, video and text data at the same time. Ogg was designed with the goal of providing a free and software patent-unrestricted alternative to proprietary formats to efficiently store and stream multimedia content.


  • Size: 40KB
